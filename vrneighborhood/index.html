Immersive Origins

VR Neighborhood

Architecture you could walk into, reshape, and share.

VR Neighborhood was a technically ambitious social VR application — a place where people could explore full-scale virtual neighborhoods, customize the spaces around them, and meet other people inside them. It shipped publicly on Meta Quest, and it became one of the foundations Edenseek is now built on.

The through-line

Architecture, then presence, then Edenseek

Edenseek didn't start from scratch. VR Neighborhood was a milestone in years of experimentation at the intersection of architecture, technology, and human connection — and its ideas lead directly into what Edenseek is building now.

Architecture

It began with a question: what if architecture wasn't something to look at, but somewhere to be? Designs meant to be inhabited, not just rendered.

↓

VR Neighborhood

That question shipped. Full-scale, customizable, multiplayer virtual neighborhoods — real-time visualization and social presence, released to the public on Meta Quest.

↓

Edenseek

The same instincts — immersion, technology, and human connection — now meet artificial intelligence and storytelling to create the next generation of interactive publishing.

The experience

A place to inhabit, not just observe

VR Neighborhood treated virtual environments as living places. Inside, visitors could:

  • Explore multiple virtual neighborhoods inspired by different architectural styles and designers.
  • Walk through buildings and environments at full scale.
  • Customize architectural materials and wall colors in real time.
  • Measure architectural spaces using built-in distance tools.
  • Experience environmental sound effects and spatial audio.
  • Socialize with other players through multiplayer interactions.
  • Customize avatars and personal homes.
  • Participate in community events.
  • Play interactive games such as chess and checkers.
  • Experience architecture as something to inhabit rather than simply observe.
The technology

Built to ship, and it did

This wasn't a concept demo. VR Neighborhood was a released product on real hardware, backed by production infrastructure.

Meta Quest · App Lab SideQuest Unreal Engine AWS GameLift

Shipped on Meta Quest

Released publicly on Meta Quest App Lab and distributed through SideQuest — installed and played on real headsets, not just in the lab.

Multiplayer at scale

An AWS GameLift backend powered real-time networking, an avatar system, and persistent player neighborhoods that were still there when you returned.

Real-time architecture

Built in Unreal Engine with live material customization, built-in measurement tools, and dynamic environmental audio — architecture rendered and reshaped on the fly.

Under the hood

  • Persistent player neighborhoods
  • Multiplayer networking
  • Avatar system
  • Real-time architectural visualization
  • Material customization
  • Measurement tools
  • Dynamic environmental audio
  • Interactive in-world activities
The legacy

The idea that became Edenseek

VR Neighborhood explored the intersection of architecture, technology, and human connection. Rather than viewing architecture as static imagery, the project treated virtual environments as living places that could be explored, customized, and shared.

Those same ideas continue today inside Edenseek, where immersive technology, artificial intelligence, and storytelling come together to create the next generation of interactive publishing.

VR Neighborhood was never simply an old game. It was an ambitious exploration of immersive architecture, multiplayer technology, and social interaction — and one of the foundations Edenseek is built on.
